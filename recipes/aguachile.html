Aguachile

Aguachile is a Mexican dish, specifically from the Sinaloa region, known for its vibrant flavors and spicy kick. It's a type of ceviche, featuring raw seafood (traditionally shrimp) "cooked" in a marinade of lime juice and chilies, served with other fresh ingredients like cucumber, red onion, and cilantro.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 ½ cups fresh lime juice
  • ½ cup chopped red onion
  • 1 serrano pepper, or more to taste, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup chopped cilantro
  • ¼ cup chopped cucumber
  • ¼ cup chopped avocado, for garnish (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Slice shrimp in half lengthwise and arrange in a single layer on a large platter.
  2. Pour lime juice evenly over shrimp.
  3. Sprinkle with red onion, serrano pepper, cilantro, and cucumber.
  4. Season with salt.
  5. Let marinate in the refrigerator for 15 to 20 minutes, or until shrimp turn opaque.
  6. Garnish with avocado, if desired, and serve immediately.
